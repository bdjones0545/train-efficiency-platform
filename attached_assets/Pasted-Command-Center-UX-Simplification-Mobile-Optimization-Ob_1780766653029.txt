Command Center UX Simplification & Mobile Optimization

Objective:

Redesign the Command Center to reduce cognitive load, improve mobile usability, eliminate duplicate decision systems, and make the page answer one question:

“What should I do next?”

This is a UX simplification project.

Do not remove functionality.

Reorganize, compress, and prioritize information.

⸻

PHASE 1 — REDUCE COMPETING PRIORITIES

Current issue:

The page contains multiple systems competing for attention:

* Attention Inbox
* Best Action Today
* Agent Quick Actions
* Business Priorities
* Agent Layer
* AI Revenue
* Revenue Recovery
* Team Training Pipeline
* Program Leads

Users are being told what to do by multiple systems.

Goal:

One primary recommendation system.

⸻

PHASE 2 — EXECUTIVE SUMMARY REDESIGN

Replace the current top area with:

TODAY

4 KPI cards:

* Revenue MTD
* Open Slots
* Pending Approvals
* Attention Items

Requirements:

* Compact cards
* Mobile-first
* Equal visual weight
* One-row scroll on mobile if needed
* Remove:
    * “24d left”
    * excessive text
    * redundant metrics

The top of the page should be readable in under 3 seconds.

⸻

PHASE 3 — SINGLE ACTION ENGINE

Keep only ONE recommendation card.

Replace:

* Best Action Today
* Agent Quick Actions

With:

RECOMMENDED ACTION

Example:

Fill Open Slot
$70 Opportunity

[Take Action]
[Why?]

Below it:

OTHER OPPORTUNITIES

Collapsed list:

* Follow up with 3 hot leads
* Review 2 outreach drafts
* Schedule 1 athlete

Expandable only when tapped.

Only one primary action should be visible.

⸻

PHASE 4 — ATTENTION SECTION

Move Attention higher.

Immediately below Recommended Action.

Display:

ATTENTION

High
Medium
Low

Example:

3 High
4 Medium
2 Low

[Open Inbox]

Do not hide attention counts behind:
“+8 more items”

This is one of the most important sections.

⸻

PHASE 5 — REVENUE RECOVERY COMPRESSION

Current cards are too tall.

Convert to compact list.

Example:

Open Slots

1:30 PM — $48
2:00 PM — $48
3:30 PM — $48

[View All]

Keep recovery value visible.

Reduce vertical space by at least 50%.

⸻

PHASE 6 — LEADS CONSOLIDATION

Merge:

* Team Training Pipeline
* Program Leads

Into one section:

LEAD PIPELINE

Top KPIs:

* Total Leads
* High Confidence
* Awaiting Approval
* Pipeline Value

Example:

117 Total
60 High Confidence
2 Awaiting Approval
$87,000 Pipeline

Then show:

Top Leads Requiring Action

Only 3–5 leads.

[View All Leads]

Do not separate pipeline metrics from lead actions.

⸻

PHASE 7 — AI WORKFORCE CONSOLIDATION

Current sections:

* Start Your Day
* Business Brain
* Today’s Business Priorities
* AI Revenue

These should become one section:

AI WORKFORCE

Display:

Business Health Score

Example:

92 / 100

Agent Status:

* Revenue Agent
* Scheduling Agent

* Support Agent

Then:

TOP FINDINGS

Example:

[Run Analysis]

Remove duplicate giant cards.

⸻

PHASE 8 — REMOVE FIXED FOOTER CTA

Current:

Ask Agent: What Should I Do Today?

appears permanently fixed at bottom.

Issue:

Consumes valuable mobile space.

Required change:

Remove the fixed footer CTA.

Keep only:

Floating Agent Button

The floating assistant already exists and provides access.

This should significantly increase usable mobile viewport height.

⸻

PHASE 9 — MOBILE OPTIMIZATION

Requirements:

* Reduce total page height
* Reduce card padding where possible
* Improve information density
* Avoid giant empty states
* Prioritize actions above analytics
* Make scrolling faster

Goal:

Users should reach Lead Pipeline in significantly fewer scrolls.

⸻

PHASE 10 — EMPTY STATE IMPROVEMENTS

Current empty states are oversized.

Examples:

Business Brain not analyzed
No pending actions
AI Revenue tracking starts now

Replace with:

Compact empty states.

Maximum height:
120px–160px

Do not use large hero cards for empty content.

⸻

⸻

PHASE 12 — SUCCESS CRITERIA

When a coach opens the Command Center:

Within 3 seconds they should know:

1. How the business is doing
2. What needs attention
3. What action creates the most value today

The page should feel:

* Cleaner
* Faster
* Less overwhelming
* More executive-focused
* Better on mobile
