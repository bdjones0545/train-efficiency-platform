# TrainEfficiency Frontend UX Audit

## Phase 1 — Adoption Module Audit

You are acting as a **Senior Product Designer, UX Researcher, SaaS Product Manager, and Sports Performance Operations Consultant**.

Your job is **NOT** to redesign the UI yet.

Your first objective is to perform a comprehensive audit of the existing Adoption module so we understand what should stay, move, merge, improve, or be removed before making any visual changes.

---

# Goal

Audit the Adoption section from the perspective of a sports performance business owner.

Assume the user owns a facility and asks:

> "What should I do today to improve athlete success, increase revenue, improve retention, and operate my business more efficiently?"

The interface should answer that question within seconds.

---

# For Every Card

Evaluate every card using the following criteria.

## 1. Purpose

* Does this feature belong on this page?
* Does it belong in this category?
* Should it live somewhere else?
* Is it redundant with another feature?

Score:
1–10

Explain why.

---

## 2. Clarity

Could a first-time customer understand:

* What this is
* Why it matters
* What it does

within five seconds?

If not, explain why.

Score:
1–10

---

## 3. Business Value

Does the card communicate measurable business value?

Examples:

Revenue

Retention

Athlete outcomes

Coach efficiency

Scheduling

Communication

Operations

If the value is unclear, explain what should be communicated instead.

Score:
1–10

---

## 4. Actionability

After reading the card:

Does the user know exactly what to do next?

Examples:

Enable

Configure

Review

Monitor

Open Dashboard

Investigate

If not, recommend a stronger call-to-action.

Score:
1–10

---

## 5. Priority

Is this feature prioritized appropriately?

Questions:

Should this be one of the first recommendations?

Is it actually high priority?

Is it medium?

Low?

Should another feature appear before it?

Score:
1–10

---

## 6. Data Quality

Audit the information displayed.

Instead of generic states like:

* Never Used
* Dormant
* Connected

Recommend actionable metrics such as:

* 12 athletes eligible
* 4 coach conflicts
* $2,300 recoverable revenue
* 5 at-risk athletes
* 8 open sessions
* 17 waitlisted athletes

Determine whether better business intelligence can be surfaced.

---

## 7. Copy Review

Evaluate all copy.

Questions:

Does it sound like internal software terminology?

Would a gym owner understand it?

Would a coach understand it?

Would a customer success manager understand it?

Rewrite only if necessary.

---

## 8. Information Architecture

Determine whether each feature belongs inside:

Customer Success

Revenue

Marketing

Operations

Scheduling

AI

Automation

Platform

Reporting

Administration

Recommend moves where appropriate.

---

## 9. Visual Hierarchy

Evaluate whether the page communicates priorities correctly.

The page should immediately answer:

What is my biggest opportunity today?

What should I review next?

What can wait?

Recommend a better hierarchy if necessary.

Return the audit as a professional UX review including:

1. Executive Summary

2. Card-by-card audit

3. Overall findings

4. Recommended structural changes

5. Quick wins

6. Medium-term improvements

7. Long-term product recommendations

Do **not** redesign the interface yet.

Be objective, critical, and prioritize business outcomes over exposing internal product features.
