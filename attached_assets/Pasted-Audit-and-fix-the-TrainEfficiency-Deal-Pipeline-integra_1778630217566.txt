Audit and fix the TrainEfficiency Deal Pipeline integration with Team Training Leads.

Current issue:
On mobile/admin Deal Pipeline, the pipeline columns say “Drop deals here.” When clicked, nothing happens. There is no obvious way for a prospect/lead to enter the deal pipeline, no visible deal cards, and no clear relationship between Team Training Leads and Deal Pipeline.

Goal:
Make Deal Pipeline a real prospect-to-client workflow, not just an empty Kanban UI.

Audit these areas:
1. Team Training Leads page
   - How leads are stored
   - Current lead statuses
   - Whether interested/contacted/won/lost states exist
   - Whether there is a “Create Deal” or “Move to Pipeline” action
   - Whether enriched/contacted leads can become pipeline deals

2. Deal Pipeline page
   - Data source for deals
   - Whether it loads deals from team training leads or a separate deals table
   - Whether empty columns are only visual placeholders
   - Whether clicking a column/card should open a deal drawer/modal
   - Whether drag-and-drop actually updates status

3. Backend/API
   - Check routes for pipeline/deals/team-training-leads
   - Confirm whether creating/updating a deal exists
   - Confirm whether lead status changes sync into pipeline
   - Add missing endpoints if needed

Expected behavior:
A Team Training Lead should be able to travel like this:

New Lead
→ Contacted
→ Interested
→ Deal Created
→ Proposal/Quote Sent
→ Follow-Up
→ Won or Lost

Deal Pipeline should show actual deal cards with:
- Organization/team name
- Contact person if available
- Email/phone if available
- Estimated deal value
- Stage
- Last contact date
- Next follow-up date
- Source lead status
- Quick actions

Required fixes:
1. Add “Move to Deal Pipeline” / “Create Deal” button on Team Training Lead cards.
2. When clicked, create a pipeline deal from that lead.
3. Prevent duplicate deals from the same lead.
4. Update the lead status to something like “deal_created” or “in_pipeline.”
5. Deal Pipeline columns should render real deal cards.
6. Empty columns should say something helpful like “No deals in this stage yet” instead of acting clickable.
7. Clicking a deal card should open a deal detail drawer/modal.
8. Dragging a deal between columns should persist the new stage to the database.
9. Won deals should update projected/won revenue cards.
10. Lost deals should stay visible in Lost/Archived or be filterable.
11. Mobile layout should be usable horizontally or convert to stacked stage sections.

Add/verify stages:
- New
- Interested
- Proposal Sent
- Follow-Up
- Won
- Lost

Also add empty-state CTA:
If there are no deals:
“Start by moving an interested team training lead into the pipeline.”
Button: “Go to Team Training Leads”

Acceptance test:
- Find or create a team training lead
- Click “Create Deal”
- Confirm it appears in Deal Pipeline under New or Interested
- Click the deal card and view details
- Drag to Proposal Sent
- Refresh page
- Confirm it remains in Proposal Sent
- Mark as Won
- Confirm Won Revenue updates
