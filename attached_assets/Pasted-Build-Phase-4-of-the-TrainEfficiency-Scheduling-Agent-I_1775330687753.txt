Build Phase 4 of the TrainEfficiency Scheduling Agent.

IMPORTANT:
Phase 1 built the org-aware scheduling foundation.
Phase 2 added intelligence and suggestions.
Phase 3 added automation and proactive scheduling behavior.

Phase 4 introduces revenue awareness, retention systems, and growth automation.

The Scheduling Agent must now understand:
- payments
- client value
- revenue per session
- retention patterns
- upsell opportunities

CORE GOAL:
Turn the Scheduling Agent into a revenue-generating and retention-driving system that actively grows the business.

---

1. STRIPE INTEGRATION (REVENUE FOUNDATION)

Integrate Stripe deeply into the platform.

Track:
- subscriptions
- one-time payments
- session packages
- remaining sessions
- billing cycles
- failed payments

Each client should have:
- payment status
- plan type
- active/inactive subscription
- session/package balance
- billing history

---

2. CLIENT LTV + VALUE TRACKING

Create a client value model:

Track:
- total revenue per client
- average spend per month
- session frequency
- lifetime value (LTV)
- retention duration

Agent should understand:
- high-value clients
- declining-value clients
- new clients vs long-term clients

---

3. CHURN RISK DETECTION

Detect when clients are likely to stop training.

Signals:
- reduced booking frequency
- missed sessions
- declining engagement
- nearing end of package
- canceled subscription

Agent outputs:
- "John has reduced his sessions by 50% this month"
- "Sarah has not booked in 10 days and may be at risk of churn"

---

4. RETENTION ACTION ENGINE

When churn risk is detected:

Agent should:
- suggest follow-ups
- suggest scheduling recommendations
- suggest offers or incentives
- suggest coach outreach

Examples:
- "Want me to suggest times for Sarah this week?"
- "John may benefit from a lower-body focus block to re-engage"

---

5. PACKAGE + SESSION MANAGEMENT

Add intelligence around session packages:

- track remaining sessions
- detect low session balances
- prompt renewals

Agent outputs:
- "Mike has 2 sessions left. Want to prompt renewal?"
- "3 clients are about to run out of sessions this week"

---

6. UPSELL ENGINE

Identify upsell opportunities:

Examples:
- move 1-on-1 → semi-private
- add additional weekly sessions
- upgrade subscription tier
- add specialty sessions (speed, recovery, etc.)

Agent outputs:
- "Sarah is consistently booking 1x/week. Moving to 2x/week could improve results and revenue"
- "This time block could support a higher-value semi-private session"

---

7. REVENUE PER TIME BLOCK

Analyze revenue by:

- day
- time
- coach
- session type

Agent should identify:
- highest-value hours
- underperforming hours

Example:
"Tuesday evenings generate 3x more revenue than mornings. Consider prioritizing premium sessions here."

---

8. PRICING + STRUCTURE INSIGHTS

Agent should recommend:

- pricing adjustments
- session restructuring
- schedule optimization for higher revenue

Example:
"Converting 3 underfilled 1-on-1 sessions into a semi-private block could increase revenue by 40%"

---

9. REVENUE DASHBOARD

Add a new section:

Revenue Intelligence Dashboard

Include:
- total revenue
- MRR (monthly recurring revenue)
- revenue per client
- revenue per coach
- revenue per time block
- churn rate
- retention rate
- average client lifetime value

---

10. AUTOMATED REVENUE ACTIONS

Connect revenue logic with automation (from Phase 3):

Examples:
- auto-notify when sessions are low
- auto-suggest bookings when engagement drops
- auto-surface upsell opportunities
- auto-flag churn risks

---

11. AGENT PROACTIVITY (GROWTH MODE)

The agent should now surface:

- "Here are 3 ways to increase revenue this week"
- "You have 5 clients at risk of churn"
- "You have $1,200 in unbooked potential revenue this week"
- "These time blocks are underperforming"

---

12. PERMISSIONS + CONTROL

Admins:
- full access to revenue + automation

Coaches:
- limited insights (their clients + schedule)

Staff:
- configurable access

---

13. FUTURE INTEGRATION READY

Ensure compatibility with:
- marketing automation
- email/SMS outreach
- affiliate systems
- white-label reporting
- corporate wellness reporting

---

PHASE 4 DELIVERABLES:

By the end of Phase 4, the Scheduling Agent should:
- understand payments and subscriptions
- track client value and LTV
- detect churn risks
- suggest retention strategies
- identify upsell opportunities
- analyze revenue across the schedule
- provide actionable growth insights
- connect scheduling directly to revenue

IMPORTANT:
This phase should make the user feel like:

"This platform helps me make more money and keep more clients."
