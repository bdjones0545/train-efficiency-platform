Audit and improve the TrainEfficiency landing page Media Library UX.

Problem:
The current Media Library has tabs like:
- Hero
- Training
- Facility

But users do not clearly understand what each upload affects. The Hero upload becomes the background media for the top landing-page hero/main slide, and there are cropping issues on mobile. The UI should make this explicit.

Implement the following:

1. Rename / clarify the media tabs:
- Hero → Main Background / Hero Slide
- Training → Training Photos
- Facility → Facility Photos

2. Update helper copy under each tab:
For Main Background / Hero Slide:
“Displayed as the large background media at the top of your public landing page. Use a wide action photo or short video. This area will be cropped differently on mobile.”

For Training Photos:
“Used in training sections, coach proof, athlete results, and service previews.”

For Facility Photos:
“Used to show your gym, equipment, space, and training environment.”

3. Add an aspect-ratio guidance card for Hero uploads:
Title: “Best for Hero Background”
Copy:
“Use wide landscape media. Recommended: 16:9 or 4:5 safe-center composition. Keep important text, faces, and logos near the center because the background crops on mobile.”

4. Add a visual crop preview after upload:
Show two previews:
- Desktop hero crop
- Mobile hero crop

The mobile preview should show how the background will appear behind the headline and CTA.

5. Improve the upload label for the Hero tab:
Change:
“Drop files here or click to upload”
To:
“Upload hero background media”

Subtext:
“This becomes the top background image or video on your landing page.”

6. Add a small warning if the uploaded hero image contains text/logos near edges:
“Text or logos near the edges may be cropped on phones.”

7. Landing page rendering:
Make sure hero background media uses:
- object-fit: cover
- object-position: center center by default
- dark overlay for text readability
- min-height that works on mobile
- no awkward top/bottom clipping of key content when possible

8. Add optional focal point controls for hero media:
Allow user to choose:
- Center
- Top
- Bottom
- Left
- Right

Store this value with the media item and apply it to object-position on the public landing page.

Goal:
The user should immediately understand that Hero/Main Background controls the top landing-page slide background, while Training and Facility uploads feed supporting sections. The public landing page should look intentional on mobile and desktop, with fewer confusing crops.
