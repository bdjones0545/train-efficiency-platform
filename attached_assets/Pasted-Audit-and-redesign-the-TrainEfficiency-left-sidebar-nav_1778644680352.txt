Audit and redesign the TrainEfficiency left sidebar navigation for coaches/admins.

Current problem:
The sidebar has evolved into a flat feature inventory instead of an intuitive business operating system. There are too many equal-weight items, AI systems are scattered, high-frequency actions are mixed with low-frequency configuration pages, and the mobile experience requires excessive scrolling.

Goal:
Reorganize the sidebar into a role-based, workflow-oriented navigation system that feels like running a business — not browsing settings.

Primary UX goals:
- Reduce cognitive overload
- Reduce mobile scrolling fatigue
- Group features by business function
- Separate operational tools from AI/system tools
- Make high-frequency actions fastest to access
- Make the platform feel cohesive

New sidebar structure:

1. HOME
This is the operational starting point.

Items:
- Command Center
- Daily Operator Mode (if separate)
- Dashboard

Notes:
- Command Center should become the primary home.
- Dashboard may eventually be merged into Command Center.

2. CLIENTS & SCHEDULING
High-frequency operational actions.

Items:
- Coaches
- Users
- Schedule
- Availability
- Group Sessions
- Team Training
- My Bookings
- Redemptions
- Transactions

3. GROWTH & REVENUE
Sales, pipeline, outreach, business growth.

Items:
- Team Training Leads
- Deal Pipeline
- Outreach Center (rename Email Agent)
- Business Plan
- Team Quotes

Future-safe area for:
- Campaigns
- Referral Engine
- Revenue Intelligence

4. AI OPERATIONS
AI orchestration + system intelligence layer.

Items:
- Business Brain
- Agent Ops Monitor
- Workflows
- Trigger Audit
- Agent Tools
- Connectors

Important:
This section should visually communicate:
“These are AI orchestration/intelligence systems.”

5. ORGANIZATION
Lower-frequency admin/configuration tools.

Items:
- Branding
- Media Library
- Stripe
- Subscription
- Options

6. DANGER ZONE
Keep isolated.

Items:
- Delete Organization

Required UX changes:

1. Collapsible accordion sections
On mobile:
- Only one major section expanded at a time
- Remember last-opened section
- Smooth expand/collapse animation
- Reduce sidebar height fatigue dramatically

2. Visual hierarchy
- Strong section headers
- Smaller text for secondary items
- Better spacing rhythm
- Distinct icon treatment for AI Operations

3. Active state improvements
- Stronger highlight for active page
- Parent section auto-expands when active route belongs to it

4. Mobile optimization
- Reduce excessive vertical spacing
- Keep logout/account anchored to bottom
- Ensure scrolling feels intentional and not overwhelming

5. Rename “Email Agent”
Rename to:
- Outreach Center
OR
- Outreach Hub

Update all references consistently.

6. Command Center positioning
Command Center should feel like the primary operational hub — likely pinned near the top under Home.

7. Do not break routing
Maintain all existing route paths and auth behavior.

Acceptance tests:
- Sidebar loads correctly for coach/admin
- Only one accordion section expanded on mobile
- Active route auto-expands parent section
- AI Operations visually grouped correctly
- Outreach Center rename reflected everywhere
- Mobile scrolling significantly reduced
- No routing regressions
