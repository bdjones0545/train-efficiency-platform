AI Operations — Next Evolution (Unified Executive Scorecard)

The recent AI Operations navigation reorganization is a major improvement. The next step is not adding more features—it is creating a unified executive scorecard at the top of AI Overview.

Objective:
Transform AI Overview from a navigation landing page into the central operating dashboard for the entire AI ecosystem.

Build a new "AI Operations Scorecard" section at the very top of AI Overview.

Cards to Include

1. Active Agents
- Running Agents
- Paused Agents
- Failed Agents
- Last Activity Timestamp

Purpose:
Provide immediate visibility into AI system activity.

2. Revenue Opportunities
- Revenue Recovery Opportunities
- Upsell Opportunities
- Estimated Pipeline Value
- Potential Revenue Impact

Purpose:
Show where AI believes money can be generated immediately.

3. Churn Risks
- High Risk Clients
- Medium Risk Clients
- Estimated Revenue At Risk

Purpose:
Provide a retention snapshot without navigating elsewhere.

4. Open Alerts
- Critical Alerts
- Warnings
- Attention Items
- Unresolved Issues

Purpose:
Centralize all actionable items.

5. Connected Integrations
- Gmail
- Calendar
- SMS
- Meta
- CRM
- Other Connected Services

Display:
Connected / Disconnected
Health Status

Purpose:
Quickly identify broken integrations.

6. AI Health Score
Calculate from:
- Agent success rate
- Workflow success rate
- Integration health
- Alert severity

Example:

AI Health Score
96 / 100

Revenue Intelligence: Healthy
Automations: Healthy
Integrations: Healthy
Alerts: 2 Active

Navigation Philosophy Audit

Ensure every AI Operations area has a clearly defined purpose:

AI Overview
"What is happening?"

Attention
"What needs action?"

Business Brain
"What should we do next?"

Automation
"What runs automatically?"

Monitoring
"Is the system healthy?"

Integrations
"What systems are connected?"

Automation Naming Audit

Review overlap between:

- Orchestration
- Automations
- Workforce
- Autonomy

Requirement:
Every section should be explainable in one sentence.

If a first-time user cannot immediately understand the difference, add:
- Section descriptions
or
- Refined labels

Example:

Orchestration
Build and manage multi-step AI workflows.

Automations
View and manage automated triggers and actions.

Workforce
Manage AI workers and responsibilities.

Autonomy
Control AI decision-making permissions and authority levels.

Design Requirements

- Use the same dark theme and visual language as the current AI Overview.
- Scorecard should be the first thing visible when opening AI Overview.
- Mobile-first responsive layout.
- Cards should collapse cleanly on smaller screens.
- Include drill-down links from every card to the related section.
- No functionality removed.
- Existing AI Operations Areas panel remains below the new scorecard.

Expected Outcome

AI Overview becomes the executive command center for the entire AI system.

Users should be able to answer these questions within 5 seconds:

- Is the AI healthy?
- Is money being left on the table?
- Are clients at risk?
- Are automations running?
- Are integrations connected?
- What needs my attention right now?

Deliver:
- Files changed
- New components created
- New metrics added
- Mobile responsiveness verification
