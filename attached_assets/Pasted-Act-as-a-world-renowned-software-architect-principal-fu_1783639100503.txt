# Page

**Coach Dashboard** under **Schedule** in the left sidebar.

This page should help coaches understand their day, manage sessions, identify schedule issues, and act quickly without unnecessary navigation.

Do not redesign TrainEfficiency.

Do not remove functionality.

Do not create mock functionality.

Improve the existing page with taste, precision, and production safety.

The interface should feel smooth, calm, fast, Apple-like, and premium while preserving TrainEfficiency’s neon green and black identity.

---

## Primary Goal

When a coach opens this page, they should immediately understand:

* What sessions do I have today?
* Who is attending?
* What needs my attention?
* Are there schedule conflicts?
* Are there underfilled or overbooked sessions?
* What actions should I take next?
* Is an AI agent recommending anything useful?

This page should feel like the coach’s operational cockpit for the day.

## Agent Intelligence & Closed-Loop Learning Assessment

Audit every intelligent workflow on this page.

### Learning Loop

Determine whether each AI capability:

* Learns from explicit coach feedback
* Learns from implicit coach behavior
* Learns from attendance patterns
* Learns from schedule changes
* Learns from no-shows, cancellations, and reschedules
* Stores learning at the correct organization scope
* Allows administrators to review learned behavior
* Allows correction or reinforcement
* Improves future recommendations

Recommend production-safe improvements where gaps exist.

---

### Closed-Loop Intelligence

Verify whether intelligent workflows include:

1. Observation
2. Recommendation
3. Human Review
4. Human Decision
5. Execution
6. Business Outcome
7. Outcome Measurement
8. Learning
9. Future Improvement

Identify missing stages and recommend safe implementation.

---

### Outcome Measurement

For every recommendation or agent-powered action determine:

* Can success be measured?
* Can failure be measured?
* Is the outcome associated with the originating recommendation?
* Are no-shows, fills, cancellations, reschedules, attendance changes, and revenue impact measured?
* Are analytics based on deterministic evidence?

Use careful wording:

* Associated outcomes
* Correlated outcomes
* Observed improvements

Do not fabricate causal attribution.

---

### Agent Memory

For every relevant agent determine:

* What should it remember about coach preferences?
* What should it remember about athlete attendance patterns?
* What should it remember about session capacity?
* What should it remember about communication preferences?
* What should it never remember?
* Is memory organization-scoped?
* Can memory be reviewed, corrected, or retired?

---

### Explainability

Every recommendation should answer:

* Why was this surfaced?
* Which schedule, attendance, capacity, or athlete data influenced it?
* What is the expected impact?
* How confident is the system, if confidence exists?
* What should the coach do next?

Do not expose internal prompts or hidden reasoning.

---

### Human-in-the-Loop

Verify that significant actions allow users to:

* Review
* Edit
* Approve
* Reject
* Save
* Regenerate
* Undo where appropriate

Important scheduling or messaging actions should not bypass coach/admin approval unless explicitly intended.
