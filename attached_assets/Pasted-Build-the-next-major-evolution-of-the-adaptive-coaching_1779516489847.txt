Build the next major evolution of the adaptive coaching system:
Intervention Prioritization Intelligence + Outcome Learning Layer.

Current state:
TrainEfficiency now:

* detects athlete state changes
* builds Athlete Context Objects
* generates adaptation drafts
* compares old vs new context
* creates coach-reviewable interventions
* surfaces drafts in Command Center

GOAL:
Move from:
“AI creates adaptation drafts”

to:
“AI understands which interventions matter most, why they matter, and whether they actually work over time.”

This phase should introduce:

1. intervention prioritization intelligence
2. intervention outcome tracking
3. intervention effectiveness learning
4. adaptive confidence scoring
5. organization-level learning patterns

PHASE 3 REQUIREMENTS

1. INTERVENTION PRIORITY ENGINE

Create:
server/services/intervention-priority-engine.ts

The engine should score all pending adaptation drafts and interventions based on:

Signals:

* readiness decline severity
* compliance deterioration
* pain/injury severity
* missed sessions
* RPE escalation
* intervention history
* athlete importance/tier
* competition proximity
* duration of issue
* multi-signal overlap

Output:
priorityScore (0–100)
priorityLevel:

* low
* medium
* high
* critical

Also generate:

* urgencyReason
* recommendedAction
* confidenceScore
* estimatedRisk

2. MULTI-SIGNAL RISK CORRELATION

Single signals should NOT be treated equally.

Examples:

* isolated low readiness for 1 day = low concern
* low readiness + missed sessions + high RPE + pain reports = critical

The engine should increase priority exponentially when multiple correlated signals appear together.

3. INTERVENTION OUTCOME TRACKING

Create table:
intervention_outcomes

Track:

* interventionId
* athleteId
* interventionType
* createdAt
* approvedAt
* completedAt
* outcomeEvaluationDate

Measure:

* readiness improvement
* compliance improvement
* RPE improvement
* pain reduction
* attendance recovery
* performance changes
* athlete engagement change

Store:

* before snapshot
* after snapshot
* delta calculations
* coach feedback
* AI effectiveness rating

4. EFFECTIVENESS LEARNING LAYER

Create:
server/services/intervention-learning-engine.ts

The system should learn:

* which interventions work best
* which athletes respond well
* which intervention timing is most effective
* which adaptation types fail most often

Examples:

* education interventions improved compliance 72% of the time
* deload adaptations reduced high-RPE states in 5 days on average
* hydration education improved readiness scores by X%

5. COMMAND CENTER PRIORITIZATION

Upgrade the Command Center.

Instead of:
“here are pending drafts”

Show:

* highest-risk athletes first
* estimated urgency
* intervention confidence
* predicted outcome impact
* recommended next action

Add:
“Why this matters now”

Example:
“Athlete has declining readiness for 9 days, rising RPE, and missed 3 sessions before playoffs.”

6. ATHLETE TRAJECTORY MODELING

Create a lightweight prediction layer.

Estimate:

* likely recovery trajectory
* intervention urgency
* projected risk escalation

Examples:

* “likely temporary fatigue”
* “high risk of disengagement”
* “possible overreaching pattern”
* “high probability compliance decline”

Do NOT use black-box ML yet.
Use explainable heuristic modeling first.

7. INTERVENTION RECOMMENDATION TYPES

Expand recommendation types beyond programming:

* deload
* simplification
* recovery emphasis
* coach conversation
* hydration education
* sleep education
* reduced frequency
* movement modification
* recovery session
* motivational outreach
* schedule adjustment

The engine should recommend the most appropriate intervention category based on signals.

8. ORGANIZATION LEARNING LAYER

Track org-level effectiveness patterns:

Examples:

* which coaches resolve risk fastest
* which interventions work best for specific age groups
* which program phases generate most fatigue
* which education pathways improve compliance

Surface this inside Business Brain and Command Center.

9. ADAPTIVE CONFIDENCE SCORING

Every recommendation should include:

* confidence score
* explanation
* evidence used

Examples:
Confidence: 84%
Reason:
“Based on similar readiness/compliance patterns across this athlete’s last 90 days.”

10. SAFETY + HUMAN CONTROL

Maintain:

* draft-first architecture
* no autonomous publishing
* coach approval required
* explainability for all recommendations

AI should recommend and prioritize —
NOT silently modify athlete programs.

11. UI UPGRADES

Add:

* Intervention Priority Queue
* Critical Athlete section
* Intervention Effectiveness Dashboard
* Outcome trend charts
* Confidence indicators
* Risk escalation timeline

12. FINAL GOAL

TrainEfficiency should evolve from:
“adaptive program recommendations”

into:
“an adaptive athlete operations intelligence system that learns which interventions improve athlete outcomes over time.”

Important:
Do NOT introduce opaque black-box AI.
Keep all reasoning explainable, auditable, and coach-reviewable.
Extend the existing:

* Athlete Context Object
* adaptation draft system
* Command Center
* WorkflowOrchestrator
* Business Brain
* TrainChat integration
