P2.2 AUDIT — Revenue Opportunity Accuracy & Trust Verification

Objective

The Revenue Opportunity Intelligence system is now live.

Before expanding automation, validate that every revenue opportunity is accurate, non-duplicative, automatically resolvable, and trustworthy.

Revenue opportunities directly influence coach behavior and business decisions.

If the inbox reports inflated revenue, duplicate opportunities, or stale opportunities, trust in the system will degrade rapidly.

This audit is focused on accuracy, resolution behavior, value estimation, and operational trust.

Do not build new signals.

Audit the existing implementation.

Revenue Signals In Scope

R1 — New Lead Never Contacted

R2 — Lead Awaiting Follow-Up 72h+

R3 — Free Intro / Assessment Completed — No Paid Conversion

R4 — Zero Upcoming Bookings This Week

Part 1 — Signal Accuracy Verification

For each revenue signal:

Provide:

* Total active items
* Sample records
* Exact query used
* Matching database records
* Signal creation timestamp
* Current status

Verify:

* Signal correctly identifies the intended business event
* Signal excludes invalid records
* Signal is scoped to the correct organization
* Signal is not firing due to stale or orphaned data

Deliver evidence for each verification.

Part 2 — Estimated Revenue Validation

Audit every estimated value calculation.

For each signal:

Document:

* Formula used
* Inputs used
* Fallback assumptions
* Missing-data behavior

Examples:

R1 — Lead Never Contacted

How is potential value calculated?

Possible sources:

* estimated_value_cents
* service price
* organization average conversion value
* static fallback

Determine which is actually being used.

R3 — Assessment Completed, No Conversion

How is estimated annual value calculated?

Verify:

* Not inflated
* Not duplicated
* Not using unrealistic assumptions

Provide:

Minimum value
Average value
Maximum value

for current production records.

Part 3 — Duplication Audit

Determine whether one business opportunity can create multiple alerts.

Examples:

Lead submitted

Can it simultaneously generate:

* R1
* R2
* activation signal
* agent recommendation
* revenue action

Assessment completed

Can it simultaneously generate:

* R3
* retention signal
* conversion signal
* recommendation

Produce:

Opportunity → Signal Matrix

Identify:

* duplicate opportunities
* overlapping alerts
* double counting

Part 4 — Resolution Audit

Verify that revenue opportunities disappear automatically when solved.

R1

Should resolve when:

* contacted_at populated
* lead converted
* lead lost

R2

Should resolve when:

* follow-up sent
* converted
* booked

R3

Should resolve when:

* paid booking created
* client purchases package
* subscription starts

R4

Should resolve when:

* confirmed booking exists within next 7 days

For each signal verify:

* automatic dismissal logic
* stale item cleanup
* orphaned item prevention

Provide evidence.

Part 5 — Revenue Inflation Audit

Determine whether estimated revenue is being counted multiple times.

Example:

Lead estimated at $297

Later:

Assessment opportunity estimated at $297

Later:

Conversion opportunity estimated at $297

Question:

Does the inbox now show:

$891 opportunity

for the same customer?

Audit:

* duplicate revenue attribution
* overlapping opportunity values
* cumulative dashboard totals

Deliver:

True opportunity value
Displayed opportunity value
Inflation percentage

Part 6 — Organization Isolation Verification

Verify for all revenue signals:

* no cross-org leakage
* no orphaned opportunities
* no stale deleted leads
* no archived prospects
* no test/demo accounts

Produce evidence.

Part 7 — Revenue Trust Score

For each signal assign:

Accuracy Score (1–10)

Questions:

* Is the event real?
* Is the timing correct?
* Is the value realistic?
* Is the action meaningful?

Example:

Signal	Accuracy	Value Accuracy	Actionability	Trust Score
R1	9	8	10	9
R2	8	8	9	8
R3	7	6	10	7
R4	10	10	9	10

Part 8 — Dashboard Integrity Audit

Review the new:

TODAY’S REVENUE OPPORTUNITIES

section.

Verify:

* sort order correct
* critical opportunities first
* estimated values render correctly
* stale opportunities removed
* CTA buttons align with opportunity type

Ensure:

The dashboard never presents misleading revenue totals.

Part 9 — Deliverables

Provide:

1. Signal accuracy report
2. Revenue calculation formulas
3. Duplication matrix
4. Resolution verification
5. Revenue inflation analysis
6. Organization isolation verification
7. Revenue trust scores
8. Dashboard integrity report
9. Production readiness score

Expected Outcome

The Revenue Opportunity system should be trusted enough that a coach can make business decisions directly from it.

The goal is:

“When TrainEfficiency says an opportunity is worth $500, that number is believable.”

Do not implement fixes yet.

Audit first.

Present evidence before making changes.
