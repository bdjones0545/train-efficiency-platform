Phase 4 Goal:
Generate outreach drafts from qualified opportunities, but do not send anything yet.

Build:

1. New service
server/services/opportunity-outreach-agent.ts

Function:
generateOutreachDraft(orgId, opportunityId)

Use:
- opportunity title
- company
- source
- type
- estimated value
- assessment reasoning
- aiCanFulfill[]
- humanRequired[]
- recommendedAction
- org name

Generate:
- subject
- body
- callToAction
- positioningAngle
- confidenceScore

Positioning should NOT say “I am applying as an AI agent” by default.

Instead frame it as:

“Your posting suggests a need for scalable programming, athlete support, and operational help. TrainChat can support this through an AI-assisted coaching system while your staff keeps ownership of relationships and final decisions.”

2. Database
Use existing opportunity_outreach_drafts table.

Fields should support:
- opportunityId
- orgId
- subject
- body
- status: draft / approved / rejected / sent
- channel: email
- confidenceScore
- createdByAgent
- approvedByUserId nullable
- sentAt nullable

3. Endpoints
POST /api/opportunity-acquisition/opportunities/:id/generate-outreach
GET /api/opportunity-acquisition/outreach-drafts
PATCH /api/opportunity-acquisition/outreach-drafts/:id

4. UI
Add new tab or section:
Outreach

If adding a new tab, tabs become:
- Discovery
- Qualification
- Outreach
- Pipeline
- Agent Activity
- Settings

Outreach tab should show:
- Draft cards
- Subject
- Body preview
- Opportunity/company
- Status badge
- Confidence score
- Buttons: Edit, Approve, Reject

5. Discovery / Qualification integration
On high-fit qualified opportunities:
Show button:
Generate Outreach

If fit score < 65:
Button disabled or warning:
“Qualification required before outreach.”

6. Activity logs
Log:
- Outreach Agent generated draft
- Outreach draft approved
- Outreach draft rejected
- Outreach draft edited

7. Important safety guard
No email sending in this phase.
No AgentMail send yet.
Only draft creation and approval workflow.

8. Draft tone
Professional, curious, not spammy.
Shorter than a traditional cover letter.
The goal is to convert job postings into software/revenue opportunities.

Example structure:

Subject:
Alternative support for your remote strength coaching role

Body:
Hi [Company/Team],

I saw your posting for [role]. The role appears to involve [need].

TrainChat may be able to support part of this workload through AI-assisted program design, athlete education, exercise modifications, and reporting.

This would not replace human relationship management, but it could reduce programming/admin load and help your staff scale.

Would it be worth a quick conversation to see if this fits what you're trying to build?

Best,
[Org Name]
